The Project

Build a website that communicates a message to a particular audience. The message can be a story, a collection of information, a how-to guide, etc. The audience can be a single person or a small group. Use your visual design skills and your new savvy of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to communicate your message in a way that can only be done using the material of the web.

Requirements:

  • Must have extensive use of custom CSS to design a unique layout
  • Must have a substantial amount of content created for the project, both text and images
  • Must use JavaScript to enable a feature that otherwise couldn’t be accomplished

Examples ideas:

  • Wishing a family member a happy birthday with a written message and a collection of memorable photographs.
  • Memorializing your favorite spot in your hometown that’s been closed down.
  • Give a list of demands to the school challenging administration to make improvements.

1) Write a proposal:

Outline who your audience is and what message you want to communicate. Define the visual language and web tools that will best fit your idea. Minimum of 500 words and 3 images.

2) Create your content:

Start writing your message and gathering your content. You may want to take photographs, scan things, find media or information online, etc. This should be an active process of authorship and research.

3) Design a draft:

Draw on paper, make a collage, use Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator, or any other process that will assist you in producing a mockup of the site, or parts of it, that you can then translate into web languages.

4) Build the site:

By now you will know the desired aesthetic of the site, have all of the content ready, know what CSS and JavaScript tricks you wish to use, and where all of these components fit together to make a website.
